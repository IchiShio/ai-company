Global Leaders Agree on New Climate Framework to Cut Emissions by 2035

Reuters · 2026/04/10 B2 中上級 読了 約4分
再生速度: 1.0x

World leaders gathered in Geneva on Thursday reached a landmark consensus on a new climate framework aimed at reducing global carbon emissions by 45% before 2035. The agreement, dubbed the "Geneva Accord," was ratified by 132 countries, marking the broadest international commitment to climate action in history.

"This is a pivotal moment for our planet," said UN Secretary-General Elena Morales, speaking at the closing plenary session. "We have moved beyond rhetoric and into tangible action."

The accord stipulates that affluent nations must provide $150 billion annually in mitigation funds to developing economies, which have historically borne the least responsibility for climate change yet face its worst effects.

Critics, however, argue that the targets remain inadequate to limit warming to 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels, warning that current trajectories still point to a 2.4°C scenario by century's end.
